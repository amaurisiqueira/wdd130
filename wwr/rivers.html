Rafiting in Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

The Salmon River, with its class I-IV rapids, promises an exciting adventure. As one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States, its water levels vary throughout the summer. Early summer brings higher, faster, and colder waters, while late summer offers lower, slower, and warmer flows. Families with children who enjoy swimming appreciate the late summer's slower pace and warmer water. Brace yourself for an exhilarating journey down the rapids of the Salmon River and create lasting memories amidst its natural splendor.

Deep in the Frank Church Wilderness of No Return lies the Salmon River. Known as the River of No Return, it captivates visitors with its rugged beauty. Surrounding the river are hillsides where herds of elk and deer graze, and sightings of bear and bighorn sheep are common. Towering ponderosa pines line the river corridor, offering sandy beaches and shelter from the elements. Immerse yourself in the untamed wilderness and tranquil ambiance of this picturesque river.
